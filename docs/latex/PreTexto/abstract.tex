\begin{abstractutfpr}%% Ambiente abstractutfpr
This work presents the development of a low-cost academic platform for creating, generating and validating High-Frequency Trading (HFT) strategies using High-Level Synthesis (HLS) through MATLAB HDL Coder. The main goal is to keep the communication infrastructure, order book, ARM--FPGA integration and validation flow previously prepared, so that the developer can focus on writing a synthesizable MATLAB strategy function. The platform targets the Intel/Terasic DE10-Nano, which combines an ARM processor in the HPS and Cyclone V programmable logic. In the proposed flow, a data-feed generator publishes synthetic FAST-encoded messages over TCP; a C++ receiver decodes the data, normalizes symbols, sides, prices and quantities, and sends 256-bit frames through MMIO; inside the FPGA, a simplified order book computes best bid, best ask, spread and imbalance; finally, the MATLAB strategy is converted to VHDL by HDL Coder, wrapped by RTL logic and integrated into the decision engine. The methodology covers the ARM--FPGA communication contract, isolation of the strategy as a replaceable block, automated HDL generation, C++ testing, VHDL simulation with GHDL and preparation of metrics for latency, throughput, FPGA resource usage and development effort. The results demonstrate the functional feasibility of the flow as a hardware/software co-design testbed and as an environment for experimenting with MATLAB-generated trading strategies, although the system is not yet a real trading platform and does not send orders to exchanges.
\end{abstractutfpr}
